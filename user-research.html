What is the most important for them to see first, second, etc.?

02. Profiles

Who we talked to:

IT Architect

“

We don't want to reinvent the wheel, we want to use whatever is already there, especially if it's in production.

What is important for me:

Save time and not develop solutions that exist from scratch, i.e. no need to take care of implementation, monitoring, evolution, support & maintenance (patching, backups...), security responsibility, deployment process...

Main challenges:

Choose solutions with long-term support and maintenance to ensure continuity. Having the responsibility for security

IT Manager

“

Je ne suis pas un spécialiste des bookmarks, donc des fois ça me prenait 20 min pour trouver le lien vers la documentation.

What is important for me:

Cost-benefit of the solution

Main challenges:

Meet the needs of the business units, while managing constraints such as budget, time, complex IT governance. The costs have an impact on the decision to reuse a solution. Documentation that is not up-to-date.

Business Manager

“

Can I see a video of how it works?

What is important for me:

DIGIT is hosting it. Costs. That the solution is maintained in the future. Easy integration with our system. Easy cooperation...

Main challenges:

Not having a real contact person but a generic mailbox.

Business Analyst

“

Comme si j'étais dans un Brico et je cherchais une perceuse

What is important for me:

Find easily if a solution exists for a functionality that I'm looking for and understand quickly what I can do with this solution, what can / should be customised, for what type of users it is designed for, etc.

Main challenges:

Communication cacophony, not having the right person to talk to.

Developer

“

Developers are lazy - the less you have to read the better.

What is important for me:

Good documentation. Support during the development. Evolution of the solution, long term support & maintenance. The responsibility for security is taken care of. Process of deployment is taken care of.

Main challenges:

Lack of visibility on the future of the solution. Forced use of emails / helpdesk instead of a real contact person.

03. User Journey

Step 01

Search for a solution by key words

Single entry point - a portal that is easy to find

Step 02

Discover the solution online

Key content

IT Architect:

  • Functionalities - what it does and what it doesn't do, understand if it matches my requirements
  • Technical specs
  • Getting started guides
  • Security information / risk assessment
  • Costs
  • What we don't need to take care of
  • Basic architecture diagram
  • Examples of how to integrate it
  • Demos
  • Some business cases - idea how big the solution and team is
  • Release plan
  • ...

Business Analyst:

  • First high-level information - what does it do, what are the functionalities, what are the possibilities that this solution offers, if it can be used for internal or external users,...
  • Second, technical specifications per requirement. Important - link between business / functional / technical information.
  • ...

IT Manager:

  • Cost
  • Functionality - what does it offer
  • Limitations
  • Contact point
  • How much time we need to set it up
  • Project Governance
  • ...

Developer:

  • Beginners summary of how it really works and then more detailed information.
  • Start with summary: is it mandatory? what do you get - what you don't need to take care of? what it does and what it doesn't do?
  • Video tutorial
  • A mock server first to start developing as quickly as possible. Give bits of code
  • Installation manual, getting started, wiki, channel for conversation
  • Information about the long term support
  • Release plan
  • If it's corporate and if it's used by others
  • ...
